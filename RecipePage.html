Mango Pickle

Mango pickle, or Aam ka Achar, is a quintessential Indian condiment made from raw green mangoes, oil, and spices. Below is a detailed recipe for a traditional, long-lasting North Indian (Punjabi) style mango pickle.

Ingredients

  • Raw Green Mangoes: 1 kg (hard, firm skin)
  • Mustard Oil: 1.5 cups (or enough to submerge, preferably cold-pressed)
  • Salt: 1/2 cup (rock salt or sea salt is best for pickling)
  • Turmeric Powder: 3 tablespoons
  • Red Chilli Powder: 1/4 cup (use Kashmiri red chili for vibrant red color)
  • Fenugreek Seeds (Methi): 1/4 cup
  • Fennel Seeds (Saunf): 1/4 cup
  • Nigella Seeds (Kalonji): 1 tablespoon
  • Asafoetida (Hing): 1 teaspoon

Instructions

  1. Prep the Mangoes: Wash the mangoes thoroughly and pat them completely dry with a clean kitchen towel.
  2. Cut & Salt: Cut them into 1-inch cubes, leaving the hard seed shell inside to keep them crunchy. In a large bowl, mix the mango pieces with salt and turmeric powder.
  3. Sun-Dry (Optional but Recommended): Spread the salted mangoes on a tray and place them in the hot sun for 1–2 days to remove excess moisture. If no sun, skip this step but ensure they are thoroughly dried with a towel.
  4. Sun-Dry (Optional but Recommended): Spread the salted mangoes on a tray and place them in the hot sun for 1–2 days to remove excess moisture. If no sun, skip this step but ensure they are thoroughly dried with a towel.
  5. Mix Everything: In a large bowl, mix the dried mango pieces with the ground spices, nigella seeds, asafoetida, red chili powder, and the remaining salt.
  6. Add Oil: Heat the mustard oil until it starts to smoke. Let it cool completely. Pour the cooled oil over the mango mixture and mix well.
  7. Store & Ferment: Transfer the mixture into a clean, sterilized glass or ceramic jar. Pour extra oil on top so it floats about 1-2 inches above the pickle.
  8. Sun-drying & Maturing: Cover the jar with a muslin cloth and leave it in the sun for 3–4 days, mixing it once daily with a dry spoon.
  9. Serve: The pickle is ready in 3-4 days, but tastes best after 10–12 days.
